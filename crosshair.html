How to improve your aim?

Improving your aim in Valorant, like in any first-person shooter (FPS), requires practice, consistency, and a systematic approach. Here are some tips to help you enhance your aiming skills:

Adjust Sensitivity Settings:

Experiment with your mouse or controller sensitivity settings to find the sensitivity that feels comfortable and precise for you. Generally, lower sensitivity (eDPI) can provide more control for aiming.

Crosshair Placement:

Keep your crosshair at head level and aimed at likely enemy positions, even when you're not in a firefight. This minimizes the distance you need to move your crosshair when an enemy appears.

Aim Training Software:

Use aim training software or maps designed for Valorant in custom games to practice your aim regularly. Aim trainers like Aim Lab, Kovaak's FPS Aim Trainer, or Valorant's in-game Aim Trainer can be helpful.

Warm-Up Routine:

Spend time warming up in the game's training range or aim training software before playing matches. Work on your tracking, flick shots, and reflexes during your warm-up.

Crosshair Settings:

Customize your crosshair settings to suit your preferences. A comfortable and visible crosshair can help you aim more accurately. Experiment with color, thickness, and length.

Play Deathmatch or Aim Training Servers:

Participate in Valorant's Deathmatch mode or join community aim training servers. These modes offer constant action and can help you practice your aim against real opponents.

How to Import Crosshair?

1. Open VALORANT and go to Settings.

2. Select the Crosshair tab on top of the Settings page.

3. Select the Import button: the third button to the right of “Crosshair Profile” with an arrow-pointed-down arrow.

4. Copy the crosshair code into the box and select Import.
